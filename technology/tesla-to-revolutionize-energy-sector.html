Tesla to revolutionize Energy Sector

-By Aditya Narayan Shahi

Written on 29thAugust,2017



      With the starting of mass production of lithium-ion batteries in Tesla’s first Gigafactory on January,2017, Elon Musk, CEO of Neuralink, Tesla and SpaceX has rattled the energy sector. Even top players in this domain haven’t dared to undertake such an ambitious project. This Gigafactory 1 (as is called) is designed to produce lithium-ion batteries which will be used in Tesla’s Model 3 (an electric car). Musk says, the whole point of the massive facility (Gigafactory 1) is to make batteries that can be used to store renewable energy like solar energy and make them affordable enough for commercial use.


      Tesla is the biggest consumer of lithium-ion batteries, hence, now wants to become its biggest producer too. Normal lithium-ion batteries with 18650 (size: 18mm X 65mm) form factor are used in laptops. Tesla’s lithium-ion batteries are unique. These new batteries have form factor greater than 18650 and referred to as ’21-70’ (size: 21mm X 70mm). Tesla uses Nickel Manganese Cobalt (NMC) lithium cells for stationary storage (Powerwall and Powerpack), and Nickel Cobalt Aluminum Oxide (NCA) for vehicles. Only Panasonic cells are to be used in Tesla model 3 cars (Panasonic is a major partner of Tesla is this sector).


      Gigafactory 1 is situated in Nevada desert. This factory is an engineering marvel in itself. While the footprint of the building is about 6 million square feet, the total planned floor space could exceed 10 million square feet. The whole facility costs about $16 million and draws power from renewable sources like geothermal and wind energy sources situated close to the factory itself. In this age of robots, Tesla will ensure a human touch by employing about 6,000 employees. Keeping in mind its environment-friendly features, Recycling (though not disclosed) will be an integral part of batteries produced inside this Gigafactory. The facility design is also peculiar. This diamond-shaped facility is aligned to true north so that the equipment can be mapped by GPS and solar panels on the roof can be accurately aligned.


      When this Gigafactory is fully operational, Tesla aims to reduce the price of its electric cars by 30% and produce upto 1.5 million cars per year. This Gigafactory will begin cell production by end of 2017.


According to Elon Musk: It would take 100 Gigafactories to power the whole world!!
