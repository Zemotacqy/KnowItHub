What is The Goldilocks Zone?

-By Karan Singh

Written on 1st September,2017



Out of billions of star systems scanned by astronomers looking for life in deep space only a few get shortlisted for further investigation. One might wonder what is the basic criteria for shortlisting an planet for further studies ? Most of the time a particular planet interests an astronomer only if it falls in The Goldilocks zone. It is not an unknown fact that there are various other planet with similar compostion to earth yet only a few have shown promise of life.


What exactly is The Goldilocks zone?


The Goldilocks zone or Circumstellar habitable zone (as wikipedia calls it) refers to the perfect range of distance between a star and its planet to sustain life .The planets lying in this zone are neither too hot nor too cold to support life. Presence of a planet in The Goldilocks zone also determine possibility of water on that planet. The zone derives its name from from the fairytale goldilocks and the three bears where she chooses the mug which is neither too big nor too small.


Whether a planet qualifies for the goldilocks basically depends upon the size of its star, its radius of orbit along with the heat flux experienced by it.

Though the concept has been in exisitence since 1953,its exact boundries have always been disputed and currently stands between 0-3 astronomical units for our solar system.


Is it just a theory?


However,some scientists still argue about the law, and consider it binded/limited by Binary systems, also this law fails to account for stellar evolutionary systems(solar systems which have an unstable stare,leading to constant change in the heat flux) where the zone can not be defined.

The law also fails to account for the planet’s own characteristics such as heat binding capability,soil characterictics, atmospheric density etc.


What Next?


Clearly given the flaws of this concept of this law it cannot be the sole condition for finding extra terrestrial life, however this is still the most accurate way to shortlist planets in our never ending search for extra terristial life.

Currently scientists are finding super earths in Goldilocks zone in our nearest star system which have possible chances of hosting life.
